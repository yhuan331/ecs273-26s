Even with a lower fair value estimate, the updated JPMorgan target of US$67 still reflects a view that Century Communities has support from the long term housing story, which some investors may see as a backing for the company’s underlying positioning in the sector. Recent research suggests analysts are not dismissing the business outright, with references to limited visibility and execution risk implying that, if conditions stabilize, there could be room for sentiment to improve as more data comes through. Century Communities lowered its full year 2026 outlook, guiding to 9,500 to 10,500 home deliveries and home sales revenues of US$3.5b to US$3.8b, citing the impact of Middle East conflict, higher gas prices, and higher interest rates on order activity. The company continued share repurchases, buying back 617,087 shares for US$40m between January 1 and March 31, 2026, bringing total buybacks under the current authorization to 2,682,502 shares for US$168.25m since July 24, 2024. The board approved a quarterly cash dividend of US$0.32 per share, a 10% increase from US$0.29. The dividend is payable on March 11, 2026 to shareholders of record on February 25, 2026. Century Communities is expanding its community footprint across states including Texas, Colorado, Georgia, Arizona, Florida, Washington, California, Kentucky, and South Carolina. The company is also continuing to promote its online homebuying platform for end to end digital transactions with support from local agents. The fair value price target moved from US$72 to US$67, a reduction of about 6.9%. The long-term revenue growth assumption shifted from 2.28% growth to a 2.49% decline. The net profit margin assumption moved from 4.42% to 3.08%. The target future P/E multiple increased from 12.0x to 18.5x. The assumed discount rate shifted from 10.63% to 9.47%, a decline of about 1.16 percentage points.
