These advances deepen Johnson & Johnson’s presence in rare disease and neuropsychiatry, areas where treatment options are limited and unmet medical need is high. To own Johnson & Johnson, you need to believe its diversified Innovative Medicine and MedTech engines can offset patent losses and legal overhangs while supporting a growing dividend. In the near term, execution on Innovative Medicine is a key catalyst, while talc litigation remains a central risk to cash flow. The new CAPLYTA relapse-prevention label and IMAAVY priority review both support the growth side of that equation, but do not remove the litigation risk. Among the recent announcements, CAPLYTA’s FDA approval for relapse prevention in schizophrenia looks most relevant. It strengthens the case that Johnson & Johnson’s acquisition of Intra Cellular Therapies can underpin its Innovative Medicine growth narrative by adding a differentiated neuropsychiatry asset with expanded clinical utility. For investors watching how Johnson & Johnson replaces revenue from older franchises, CAPLYTA’s broader label is a tangible proof point within the newer portfolio rather than a theoretical pipeline story.
