The U.S. Department of Justice antitrust division is reported to be running a criminal investigation into major meatpackers, with Tyson Foods among the publicly traded companies under scrutiny, according to the Wall Street Journal. Reports indicate the DOJ probe is focused on beef companies, including large publicly traded processors such as Tyson Foods, as part of a broader review of competition in the meat sector. Senate Democrats are preparing legislation that would break up large U.S. meatpacking companies. The proposal could prevent firms from processing more than one type of meat and may require processors like Tyson Foods to spin off beef plants.
