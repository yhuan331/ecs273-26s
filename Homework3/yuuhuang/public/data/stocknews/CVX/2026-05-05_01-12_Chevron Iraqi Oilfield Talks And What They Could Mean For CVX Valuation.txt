Chevron, ticker NYSE:CVX, is in talks to acquire stakes in major Iraqi oilfields, potentially taking over positions held by Russian companies. The negotiations center on large producing fields that are important to Iraq's output and to global oil supply. Any agreement could adjust the balance of international players in Iraq's energy sector and influence regional energy ties among the U.S., Russia and China. For investors watching NYSE:CVX, this development comes with the stock trading around $192.28 and having recorded a 23.3% total return year to date and 48.3% over the past year. The stock has also returned 116.1% over the past five years, with shorter term moves such as a 4.1% gain over the past week and a 3.4% decline over the past month. If Chevron secures sizeable Iraqi positions, the company could gain exposure to some of the world's larger reserves while widening its Middle East footprint. For readers, key factors to monitor include the size and terms of any finalized stakes, how these assets fit into Chevron's existing portfolio and what this might indicate about longer term U.S. energy interests in the region.
