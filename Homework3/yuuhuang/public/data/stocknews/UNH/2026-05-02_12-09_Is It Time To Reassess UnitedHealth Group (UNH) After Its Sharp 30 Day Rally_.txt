A Discounted Cash Flow, or DCF, model estimates what a business could be worth today by projecting its future cash flows and discounting them back to the present. For UnitedHealth Group, the model used is a 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity approach, based on cash flows reported and projected in $. The latest twelve month free cash flow is about $19.3b. Analyst estimates and extrapolations suggest projected free cash flow of $30.3b in 2030, with interim annual projections between 2026 and 2035 ranging from around $18.4b to $41.1b before discounting. After discounting these projected cash flows, the DCF model arrives at an estimated intrinsic value of $891.52 per share. Compared with the current share price of about $368.78, this implies the stock is roughly 58.6% undervalued based on these cash flow assumptions. On this view, the current price sits well below the model’s estimate of underlying value. For profitable companies, the P/E ratio is a useful way to see how much you are paying for each dollar of earnings, which often lines up more directly with shareholder returns than sales or book value. A higher P/E can reflect stronger growth expectations or lower perceived risk, while a lower P/E can point to more modest growth assumptions or higher uncertainty. UnitedHealth Group currently trades on a P/E of 27.8x, compared with the Healthcare industry average of about 24.9x and a peer average of 31.5x. On its own, this suggests the market is applying a valuation that sits between the broader industry and closer peers. Simply Wall St’s Fair Ratio framework goes a step further. It estimates what a “fair” P/E might be for UnitedHealth Group by factoring in elements such as earnings growth, profit margins, industry, market cap and company specific risks. For UnitedHealth Group, the Fair Ratio is 41.7x, which is meaningfully above the current 27.8x P/E. On this measure, the shares screen as undervalued relative to the Fair Ratio output.
